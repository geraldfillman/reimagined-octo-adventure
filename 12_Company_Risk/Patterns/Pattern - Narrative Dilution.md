# Pattern: Narrative Dilution

## Description

Management consistently emphasizes forward-looking metrics, adjusted figures, or TAM narratives while burying unfavorable structural data in footnotes, earnings supplements, or non-GAAP reconciliations. The gap between the headline story and the underlying financials widens over time.

## Signals

- Heavy use of non-GAAP metrics without clear GAAP reconciliation
- Revenue recognition changes that inflate near-term results
- Guidance couched in "bookings," "ARR," or pipeline language rather than recognized revenue
- Frequent reference to total addressable market without segment-level performance disclosure
- Declining gross margins explained away with one-time charges quarter after quarter
- Increasing complexity of earnings supplements relative to core 10-Q/10-K filings
