## Definition
A period where the central bank is actively lowering the federal funds rate to stimulate economic activity. Identified by consecutive rate cuts, dovish forward guidance, and falling short-term yields.

## Sector Impact Map
### Favored
- [[Tech Sector]] — lower rates boost growth stock valuations
- [[Real Estate]] — mortgage rates fall, refinancing surge
- [[Consumer Discretionary]] — cheaper credit fuels spending

### Challenged
- [[Financials]] — net interest margins compress

## Historical Precedents
- 2024-2025: Post-inflation normalization
- 2019: Insurance cuts
- 2007-2008: Emergency cuts during financial crisis

Evaluate Fed stance and market pricing for future cuts.
