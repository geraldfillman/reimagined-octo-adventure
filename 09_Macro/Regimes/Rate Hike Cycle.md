## Definition
A period where the central bank is actively raising the federal funds rate to combat inflation. Identified by consecutive rate increases at FOMC meetings, hawkish forward guidance, and rising short-term yields.

## Sector Impact Map
### Favored
- [[Financials]] — wider net interest margins
- [[Energy]] — often coincides with strong demand

### Challenged
- [[Tech Sector]] — higher discount rates compress growth valuations
- [[Real Estate]] — mortgage rates rise, affordability drops
- [[Consumer Discretionary]] — higher borrowing costs reduce spending

## Historical Precedents
- 2022-2023: Fastest hiking cycle in 40 years (0% → 5.25%)
- 2015-2018: Gradual normalization cycle
- 2004-2006: Greenspan measured pace
