## Thesis Statement
The U.S. Department of Defense is undergoing the most significant technological transformation since precision-guided munitions — shifting from platform-centric warfare to AI-enabled, software-defined operations. Ukraine has stress-tested AI targeting, ISR fusion, and drone swarm coordination at scale, validating every major program under development. The JWCC cloud contract ($9B), Maven Smart System, IVAS, and dozens of DARPA/SOCOM programs are funneling multi-billion dollar annual contract flows to a concentrated set of software-first defense companies. This is a structural, multi-decade spend cycle — not a cyclical uptick.

## Core Logic
1. **Software eats the Pentagon**: DoD's FY2025 AI/autonomy budget exceeded $1.8B; projected to compound at 15%+ CAGR through 2030 as platforms become increasingly autonomous
2. **Ukraine as validation lab**: AI-enabled targeting (Kropyva, Delta), drone swarm coordination, and real-time ISR fusion proved decisive — every NATO member is now replicating
3. **JWCC cloud dominance**: MSFT + AMZN split the $9B Joint Warfighting Cloud Capability — the infrastructure layer underpinning every DoD AI application
4. **Palantir's Maven moat**: PLTR's AI Platform (AIP) is now embedded in targeting, logistics, and battlefield C2 — switching costs approach infinity once integrated
5. **Counter-AI as new category**: As adversaries deploy AI-enabled systems, counter-AI, EW, and cyber become mandatory — doubling the addressable market
6. **Second-order contractor pull-through**: Prime integrators (LDOS, BAH, SAIC, CACI) absorb billions in AI subcontracts — less binary risk than pure-plays
