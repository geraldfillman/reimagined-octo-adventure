## Thesis Statement
The biology of aging is the largest scientific problem being tackled with serious capital in the 2020s. Jeff Bezos, Sam Altman, Larry Page, and Peter Thiel have collectively deployed $3B+ into longevity companies (Altos Labs, Calico, NewLimit, Unity Biotechnology). The Hallmarks of Aging framework (Lopez-Otin et al.) has provided a scientific consensus roadmap. FDA is reviewing whether to treat aging as a disease indication (the TAME trial sets precedent). The public market opportunity is early — most companies are private and pre-clinical. But the signals are available now in the research data: arXiv q-bio, PubMed, and clinical trial registrations are the canary.

**Important note**: This is a **low-conviction, long-dated** thesis. Position sizing should reflect the scientific uncertainty. The primary value is as a *signal thesis* — monitoring the research to identify when the private-to-public transition happens (IPOs, SPAC mergers) and which approaches are winning.

## Core Logic
1. **The Hallmarks of Aging are a tractable target list**: 12 identified hallmarks (genomic instability, telomere attrition, epigenetic alterations, loss of proteostasis, deregulated nutrient sensing, mitochondrial dysfunction, cellular senescence, stem cell exhaustion, altered cell-to-cell communication) each offer druggable targets. This is scientific maturity, not speculation.
2. **Cellular senescence is the most advanced target**: Senescent cells (zombie cells that refuse to die) accumulate with age and secrete SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype) inflammatory factors. Senolytics (drugs that clear senescent cells) have shown efficacy in mouse aging models. Unity Biotechnology (UNTY) is in Phase 2.
3. **Epigenetic reprogramming is the most transformative**: Steve Horvath's epigenetic clock showed biological age is measurable and reversible. Altos Labs (Bezos, $3B) is pursuing partial reprogramming — resetting cells to a younger epigenetic state without dedifferentiation. Phase 1 trials expected 2026–2027.
4. **mTOR/rapamycin is the most validated intervention**: Rapamycin extended lifespan in 18 mouse strains; now being studied in dogs (Dog Aging Project) and humans (PEARL trial). mTOR inhibitors are already FDA-approved (for cancer) — compassionate use off-label prescribing is growing.
5. **GLP-1 metabolic connection is accelerating longevity interest**: Metabolic dysfunction is Hallmark #5 (deregulated nutrient sensing). GLP-1's cardiovascular and metabolic benefits directly intersect with aging biology — the drugs are effectively longevity interventions in metabolic populations.
6. **The IPO wave will come**: When Altos Labs, Calico, or NewLimit produces Phase 2 data, they will access public markets. The arXiv and PubMed signals are the earliest warning system — publication velocity from these groups precedes S-1 filing by 12–24 months.
