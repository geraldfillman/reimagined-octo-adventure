## Thesis Statement
Drones and autonomous aerial systems are experiencing a structural inflection point across military, commercial, and urban air mobility domains. The Ukraine war has proven drones as the dominant battlefield technology, triggering $100B+ in global defense drone procurement. Simultaneously, FAA/EASA eVTOL certification is accelerating toward commercial air taxi services by 2026-2027. This thesis spans 4 segments — from combat-proven military systems to sci-fi eVTOL air taxis — unified by the common thread of autonomous aerial platforms reshaping defense, logistics, and transportation.

## Core Logic
1. **Ukraine as live laboratory**: 2.5-4M drones produced in 2025, targeting 7M in 2026 — battlefield data is reshaping global procurement doctrine
2. **Defense budget supercycle**: EU SAFE $178B, India +15%, NATO 2%+ GDP, US Drone Dominance 300K+ UAS
3. **Supply chain nationalism**: NDAA Section 1709 bans Chinese drones (DJI), creating domestic manufacturing opportunity
4. **Counter-UAS as new category**: ~$7.5B US alone in 2026 — every drone threat creates counter-drone demand
5. **eVTOL certification momentum**: Joby ~70% through FAA cert, Archer 100% MOC accepted, EHang already flying passengers
6. **AI convergence**: Autonomous swarm coordination, EW, and target recognition turning drones from tools into intelligent systems
