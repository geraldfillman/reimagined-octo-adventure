## Thesis Statement
GLP-1 receptor agonists represent the largest new drug class in the history of medicine by addressable patient population. With 42% of US adults obese and downstream cardiovascular, metabolic, and liver disease adding hundreds of millions globally, GLP-1s are not a trend — they are a platform. First-generation injectable dominance (Ozempic, Mounjaro) is giving way to oral formulations, combination agents, and tissue-specific variants that will reshape diabetes, NASH, heart failure, and potentially Alzheimer's treatment paradigms through 2035.

## Core Logic
1. **Market size without historical precedent**: Obesity (BMI ≥30) affects ~650M adults globally. At $15–20K/year US list price, theoretical penetration of even 10% = $1T annual market. No prior drug class approaches this TAM.
2. **Cardiovascular outcomes lock in reimbursement**: SURMOUNT-MMO (tirzepatide), SELECT (semaglutide) cardiovascular outcome trials showed 20–26% MACE reduction. Cardiologists now prescribe GLP-1s — removes prior obesity stigma in prescribing.
3. **Oral pipeline is the next unlock**: VKTX VK2735 oral Phase 3 data expected 2026. If oral matches injectable efficacy, addressable patient volume multiplies — needlephobia is a top adoption barrier.
4. **NASH/liver fibrosis is the next FDA approval wave**: 4M+ Americans have NASH with significant fibrosis; resmetirom (Rezdiffra) approved 2024, GLP-1 combos next. ALVO's pemvidutide targets liver directly.
5. **Compounding pressure is temporary but real**: Section 503B compounders producing tirzepatide/semaglutide copies — FDA shortage designation expires, compounding ends. Near-term pricing pressure resolves in 2025–2026.
6. **AMGN's MariTide (AMG 133) is the differentiated challenger**: GIP/GLP-1 bispecific antibody, monthly dosing, potentially superior weight loss. Phase 3 readout creates binary event risk/opportunity vs. Lilly.
