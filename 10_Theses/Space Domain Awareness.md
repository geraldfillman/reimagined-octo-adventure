## Thesis Statement
The militarization of low Earth orbit is the defining strategic competition of the next decade. China has demonstrated kinetic ASAT (anti-satellite) weapons, directed energy capabilities, and is deploying a 13,000-satellite constellation. The US Space Force (established 2019, $30B+ annual budget) is the fastest-growing military branch, and its doctrine has shifted from exquisite expensive satellites (one $3B asset you can't lose) to proliferated LEO architectures (1,000 cheap satellites — shoot one down, the constellation adapts). This creates a massive procurement wave for small satellites, launch, ISR data services, and space domain awareness platforms.

## Core Logic
1. **Proliferated LEO is the DoD doctrine**: Space Development Agency (SDA) has awarded $5B+ in contracts for the Proliferated Warfighter Space Architecture (PWSA) — hundreds of small satellites for missile tracking, communications, and navigation resilience. This is the end of the exquisite satellite era.
2. **Rocket Lab is the most credible commercial launch alternative to SpaceX**: Electron rocket has 50+ successful launches. Neutron medium launch vehicle targets the government mission market directly. RKLB's Photon spacecraft bus is an integrated satellite platform — turning RKLB from a launch company into a space systems company.
3. **Planet Labs provides the commercial ISR data layer**: PL's 200+ Dove satellites provide daily imagery of every point on Earth at 3m resolution. Defense and intelligence customers are the fastest-growing segment — classified contracts protect against commercial revenue cyclicality.
4. **AST SpaceMobile is the space-to-cell moat**: ASTS is building a broadband constellation that talks directly to unmodified smartphones. T-Mobile partnership + FCC approval = first commercial direct-to-device satellite broadband service. DoD battlefield connectivity use case is enormous.
5. **Space domain awareness is the ISR of space itself**: BlackSky (BKSY) and Spire (SPIR) provide geospatial and radio frequency intelligence from orbit. As adversary satellite proliferation grows, knowing what they're doing becomes mission-critical. These are the sensors for the space battlefield.
6. **SDA contract awards are the revenue catalyst**: Each SDA Tranche award ($100M–$1B) to a commercial player is a multi-year revenue commitment. Watch for Tranche 2/3 awards — they validate commercial architecture viability.
